Non-Basmati Rice

India also exports several non-basmati rice varieties, which make up the majority of its rice exports:

1. Parboiled Rice

Processed to retain nutrients, with a golden hue. Popular in African and Southeast Asian countries.

2. Sona Masoori

Medium-grain rice with a mild flavor. Primarily exported to the U.S., Europe, and the Middle East.

3. IR64 & IR36

Long-grain, low-cost rice varieties. Commonly exported to African and Asian countries.

4. Ponni Rice

Short-grain rice, ideal for South Indian dishes. Exported to Southeast Asia and the Middle East.

5. Jeera Samba

Short-grain aromatic rice, popular for traditional dishes. Exported to Gulf and Asian countries.

6. Matta (Red) Rice

Nutrient-rich rice with a reddish-brown hue. Exported to Sri Lanka, the Middle East, and parts of Europe.

Specialty Varieties

1. Black Rice

Rich in antioxidants, with a nutty flavor. Exported to health-conscious markets in Europe and North America.

2. Sticky Rice (Glutinous Rice)

Used in desserts and Asian cuisines. Exported to Southeast Asian markets.
