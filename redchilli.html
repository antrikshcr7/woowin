Mathania Mirchi, named after the Mathania region in Jodhpur, Rajasthan, is known for its deep red color, moderate spiciness, and unique flavor, making it a sought-after ingredient in Rajasthani cuisine.

Geographical Identification (GI) of Mathania Mirchi:

  • Region: Grown primarily in Mathania and surrounding areas in Jodhpur district, Rajasthan.
  • Climate & Soil: The arid climate and sandy soil of Rajasthan contribute to its distinctive flavor and deep red hue.
  • Taste & Aroma: Unlike extremely spicy chilies, Mathania Mirchi offers a balanced heat with rich flavor, making it ideal for traditional curries and Rajasthani dishes.
  • Traditional Cultivation: Farmers in Mathania have been growing this chili for generations, using traditional methods that enhance its unique characteristics.
  • GI Tag Status: Efforts are underway to obtain a Geographical Indication (GI) Tag to protect its authenticity and increase market value.

Why is a GI Tag Important?

  • Protects the authenticity and prevents counterfeit production.
  • Increases the economic value and international recognition.
  • Benefits local farmers by ensuring fair pricing and branding.
